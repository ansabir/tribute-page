Jamset Ji Tata

He had four goals in life: setting up an iron and steel company, a world class learning institution, a unique hotel and a hydro-electric plant.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 3 March 1839 Jamsetji Tata was Born
  • 1858 Graduated from Elphinsten College
  • 1868 Started a trading company with 21000 Indian Rupees capital
  • 1869 He bought a bankrupt oil mill at Chinchpokli and convert it into cotton mill, which he renamed as Alexandra Mill.
  • 1874 He floated the Central India Spinning, Weaving and Manufacturing Company in Nagpur.
  • 3 December 1903 He inaugrated The Taj Mahal Hotel at Colaba, only hotel to have electricity.
  • 19 May 1904 He died in Bad Nauheim and was buried in the Parsi burial ground in Brookwood Cemetry, Working, England.

His successors' work let to the three remaining ideas being achieved.

  • Tata Stell(formerly TISCO - Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited) is Asia's first and India's Largest steel company after it acquired Coros Group producing 28 million tonnes of steel annually.
  • Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, the pre-eminent Indian institution for research and education in Science and Engineering.
  • Tata Hydroelectric Power Supply Company renamed Tata Power Company Limited, currently India's largest private electricity company with an installed generation capacity of over 8000MW.
